NOTE TO SELF:

When you were in mycorrhiza it was possible for you to work very productively on projects for weeks at a time. There were definetly periods of time where you were able to put in 12 hour days, and pump out massive quantities of work.

However this really was never consistent, there were absolutely periods where things were really good, like the last 5 months of working on kessler, and december/early january of seance. And things are even worse now, where for the last 2 weeks you have been almost incapable of getting anything done, despite having a super important and cool project to work on.

Aside from the basic shit like making sure you are exercising and taking care of basic mental health stuff like:
- Sleep
- Exercise
- Food
- Socializing

But in general, if you are failing to work effectively on a project it is always caused by your brain not being able to exert enough force in the form of motiviation to overcome the friction of not wanting to work.The big issue with startups is that you essentially have infinite motivation, they are insanely stressful experiences, and because you put so much of yourself into the company its easy to find work that you can obsess over. 

This is all a trap though. Using the high motivation inherent in a startup to get you to work is a uniquely horrible idea for all the following reasons:

## When the world needed you most, you didn't have the motivation.

## You need to get better at things you don't find fun right now.

## Burning the company down for the sake of you feeling productive.
Everyone has this innate desire to "be productive", this feeling tends to influence our decisions to work on things that invoke the sensation of feeling productive. When working on something like a thesis this might mean that you spend most of your time tweaking font settings instead of actually writing it.

But when you run a business, there are infinite amount of things you could be doing, but only a small portion of those are going to be things that are crucial to the success of the buisness going forward.
